Habitat

Welsh Corgis are domesticated dogs that thrive in hilly and mountainous areas12. They are natural herding dogs and are happiest on farms, but a roomy apartment with an outdoor space would be fine too3. The ideal habitat for this breed is one that offers plenty of space for playtime, regular walks, and outdoor activities such as running and chasing a ball or frisbee4. Typically, these dogs do better in cooler climates due to the thick fur coats that can make them overheat easily when exposed to heat for long periods of time4.
